- **Pro:** Save - per patent
- **Con:** 50%+ higher rejection rate
- **Recommendation:** Use attorney for patents, DIY for trademarks --- ### Q: What's the difference between ™ and ®? **A:**
- **™** = Common law trademark (can use immediately, no registration)
- **®** = Registered trademark (only after USPTO approves) **Rule:** Use ™ now, switch to ® when registered --- ### Q: How long until patents approve? **A:** Typical timeline:
- Provisional: 12 months (then must convert)
- First Office Action: 12-18 months after filing
- Final approval: 2-4 years from filing
- Can use "Patent Pending" immediately --- ### Q: What if someone copies my technology before patent approves? **A:**
- If they copy AFTER your filing date: You can sue when patent approves (backdate damages)
- If they copied BEFORE your filing date: They may have prior art defense
- **Mitigation:** File quickly, document everything with dates --- ### Q: Can I change patent claims after filing? **A:**
- **Yes:** During prosecution, can amend claims in response to Office Actions
- **Limitations:** Can't add "new matter" (new inventions)
- **Strategy:** Start with broad claims, narrow if needed --- ### Q: What if trademark gets opposed? **A:**
- **Probability:** 10-15% for distinctive marks
- **Process:** 30-day opposition period after publication
- **Resolution:** Usually settle with coexistence agreement
- **Backup:** Can amend mark or file in different classes --- ### Q: Should I file internationally right away? **A:**
- **If well-funded:** Yes, file PCT (Patent) + Madrid (Trademarks) for global coverage
- **If bootstrapped:** File US first, expand within 12 months (use priority date)
- **Minimum:** US (largest market) + EU (second largest) --- ## 📞 SUPPORT **Questions about:**
